## Motivation

In stochastic systems such as diffusion, time is often treated as an
external parameter rather than an observable quantity.

This work asks a precise operational question:

> *What is the minimal time difference that can be distinguished
> from data generated by stochastic dynamics?*

The answer turns out to be constrained not by metaphysics or new physics,
but by **information theory**.

---

## Core Idea

Time is treated as an *inferred parameter* of probability distributions,
not as a primitive variable.

Temporal resolution is limited by:
- statistical sample size,
- measurement noise,
- the dynamical law governing state evolution.

This leads to sharp, estimator-independent bounds on temporal
distinguishability.
